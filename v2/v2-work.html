Discovery & Solution Design

Enterprise Assessment Platform

Context: An institutional client needed a scalable digital assessment framework but lacked clear requirements and defined operational workflows.

Problem: Transitioning from fragmented, offline assessment processes to a unified digital platform without disrupting existing institutional operations.

Users: Institutional leaders, content authors, and internal engineering teams.

My Role: Contributor to Product Discovery & Solution Design.

Approach: Conducted product discovery sessions and contributed to detailed requirement documentation. Acted as a bridge between institutional stakeholders and the technical team, collaborating closely with engineering leads to ensure workflow feasibility.

Constraints & Trade-offs: Balanced the client's desire for a rapid go-to-market launch against the necessity of building a scalable taxonomy. Recommended prioritizing governance standards before platform development to mitigate risks of technical debt.

Solution: Co-authored a comprehensive Business Requirements Document (BRD) and delivery blueprint that translated institutional needs into actionable engineering requirements.

Impact: Supported stakeholder alignment and provided a clear roadmap for engineering. Success was evaluated through smoother UAT cycles without critical blockers and a measurable increase in operational efficiency during content upload.

Learning: Clear taxonomy and operational alignment are prerequisites for any scalable EdTech product.

Product Operations & Adoption

Designing Learning Technology Experiences for Enterprise Adoption

Context: A global workforce was experiencing suboptimal engagement and retention with their existing digital learning delivery.

Problem: A widespread LMS implementation that was rich in features but poorly aligned with actual learner workflows and operational realities.

Users: Global workforce learners, Subject Matter Experts (SMEs), and learning designers.

My Role: Senior Learning Designer & Operations Lead.

Approach: Analyzed learning analytics to identify drop-off points. Collaborated with instructional designers and platform developers to define behavioural workflows and support execution.

Constraints & Trade-offs: Highlighted the tension between feature-heavy content requests from SMEs and learner cognitive load. Advocated for usability by promoting streamlined, micro-learning workflows over exhaustive info-dumping.

Solution: Supported the optimization of the LMS implementation by incorporating standardized design frameworks and data-informed user paths to drive adoption.

Impact: Contributed to a ~15% measurable uplift in learner retention. Success was evaluated through module completion rates (adoption), session duration (engagement), and qualitative user feedback indicating reduced platform fatigue.

Learning: The most powerful platform features fail if they don't align with the user's daily operational reality.

Data Architecture & Frameworks

Designing Structured Assessment Frameworks for Education Ecosystems

Context: A requirement to evaluate teacher performance rigorously against national accreditation standards.

Problem: Moving from subjective evaluations to a standardized, scalable, and data-driven assessment lifecycle.

Users: Educators, accrediting bodies, and cross-functional assessment teams.

My Role: Assessment Lifecycle Contributor.

Approach: Translated complex accreditation rules into a structured assessment blueprint. Contributed to logic requirements and supported rigorous validation/UAT to ensure accurate analytics reporting.

Constraints & Trade-offs: Navigated the balance between rigid national accreditation rubrics and the need for a user-friendly evaluation system. Recommended capturing essential compliance data points while abstracting the complex rule engine away from the end-user.

Solution: Supported the design of a scalable assessment framework spanning from initial blueprinting through to analytics and reporting.

Impact: Helped generate clear performance data and actionable insights. Success was defined by the accuracy of quality indicators and the efficiency of generating performance reports for stakeholders.

Learning: EdTech data is only valuable when the underlying framework is structured to drive specific stakeholder actions.
